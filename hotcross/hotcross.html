Hot Cross Buns

Hot Cross Buns are a staple food in the Uk over Easter. It is thought to have originated from an English monk in the 12th century who decorated his freshly baked buns with a cross on Good Friday, also known as the Day of the Cross. The custom gained traction, and over the years, fruits and precious spices were included to represent health and prosperity.

Prep Time

25 mins

Total Time

2hrs 45 mins

Servings

12 Person

Ingredients

  • 3 teaspoons instant or rapid rise yeast
  • 1/2 cup caster sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups warm milk
  • 4 1/4 cups bread flour
  • 2 tsp cinnamon powder
  • 2 tsp All Spice
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cups sultanas
  • 1 – 2 oranges, zest only
  • 3.5 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 egg
  • 1tbsp apricot jam

Instructions

  1. Mix The Dry Ingredients

    Place flour, yeast, sugar, all spice, cinnamon, and salt in a large bowl. Briefly mix with stand mixer fitted with a dough hook.

  2. Add The Wet Ingredients

    Add butter, milk, egg, sultanas and zest.

  3. Mixing

    Mix for 5 minutes until a smooth elastic dough forms. Start on speed 2 then once the ingredients are combined, increase to speed 4. After 1 minute, add extra flour if required, just enough so dough comes away from side of bowl when mixing and doesn't stick terribly to your fingers.

  4. Leave To Rise

    Leave dough in the bowl, cover with cling wrap and place in a warm, wind free place to rise until doubled in size. This will take anywhere between 30 minutes to 1 1/2 hours depending on how warm it is.

  5. Forming Balls

    Line a 31.5 x 23.5 cm / 9 x 13″ tray with baking paper with overhang. Remove cling wrap and punch dough to deflate. Dust work surface with flour, place dough on work surface, shape into a log - this will deflate the air. Cut into 12 equal pieces. Take one piece and press down with palm, then use your fingers to gather into a ball, then roll the dough briefly to form a ball. This stretches the dough on one side and that’s how I get a nice smooth surface. Place the ball with the smooth side up on the tray. Repeat with remaining dough. Line them up 3 x 4.

  6. Rise #2

    Line a 31.5 x 23.5 cm / 9 x 13″ tray with baking paper with overhang. Spray a piece of cling wrap lightly with oil (any), then loosely place over the tray. Return tray to warm place and leaver 30-45 minutes, until the dough has risen by about 75% (less than double in size). Partway through Rise #2, preheat oven to 180°C/350°F (all oven types).


  7. Crosses

    Mix flour and water until a thick runny paste forms - see video for thickness required. Spoon into a round 3 mm piping bag or small ziplock bag then snip corner. Remove the cling wrap and pipe crosses onto the buns. Go slow so it hugs the curves.

  8. Bake and Glaze

    Bake in preheated oven (180°C/350°F) for 22 minutes, or until the surface is a deep golden brown. The surface colour is the best test for this recipe. Meanwhile, place jam and water in a bowl, microwave for 30 seconds. Mix to combine. Remove buns from oven. Use overhang to lift buns onto a cooling rack. Brush with jam mixture while warm. Allow to cool to warm before serving.
